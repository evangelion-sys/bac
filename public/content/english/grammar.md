## 1. Tenses (The Time Travelers)

Knowing when an action happens is crucial.

### Present Perfect vs. Past Simple
*   **Past Simple:** An action that started and finished in the past at a specific time.
    *   *Keywords:* yesterday, last year, in 2010, ago.
    *   *Example:* I **visited** London last summer.
*   **Present Perfect:** An action that started in the past and continues to the present, or an action with a result in the present (time is not specific).
    *   *Rule:* have/has + past participle
    *   *Keywords:* since, for, already, yet, just, recently.
    *   *Example:* I **have lived** here for five years. (I still live here).

### Past Perfect (The Past of the Past)
Used when two actions happened in the past, to show which one happened first.
*   *Rule:* had + past participle
*   *Keywords:* after, before, by the time, as soon as.
*   *Example:* **After** she **had finished** her homework (1st action), she **watched** TV (2nd action).

## 2. Active and Passive Voice (The Transformers)

This is a classic BAC transformation exercise!

*   **Active:** The subject does the action. (The cat ate the mouse).
*   **Passive:** The subject receives the action. Focus is on the action, not who did it. (The mouse was eaten by the cat).

### How to transform:
1.  Object becomes Subject.
2.  Verb "to be" takes the tense of the main verb in the active sentence.
3.  Main verb becomes Past Participle.
4.  Subject becomes Object (introduced by "by").

*   **Present Simple:** The company **produces** cars. $\to$ Cars **are produced** by the company.
*   **Past Simple:** Shakespeare **wrote** Hamlet. $\to$ Hamlet **was written** by Shakespeare.
*   **Present Continuous:** They **are building** a house. $\to$ A house **is being built**.
*   **Present Perfect:** He **has finished** the project. $\to$ The project **has been finished**.
*   **Modals:** You **must wear** a uniform. $\to$ A uniform **must be worn**.

## 3. Direct and Reported Speech (The Messengers)

How to report what someone else said.

### The Rules of Transformation:
When the reporting verb (e.g., "said", "told") is in the **past tense**, the tense inside the quotes goes one step back into the past.

*   **Present Simple $\to$ Past Simple:**
    *   Direct: "I **like** apples," she said.
    *   Reported: She said that she **liked** apples.
*   **Present Continuous $\to$ Past Continuous:**
    *   Direct: "I **am reading**," he said.
    *   Reported: He said that he **was reading**.
*   **Past Simple $\to$ Past Perfect:**
    *   Direct: "I **went** to Paris," she said.
    *   Reported: She said that she **had gone** to Paris.
*   **Present Perfect $\to$ Past Perfect:**
    *   Direct: "I **have finished**," he said.
    *   Reported: He said that he **had finished**.
*   **Will $\to$ Would:**
    *   Direct: "I **will call** you," she promised.
    *   Reported: She promised that she **would call** me.

### Questions in Reported Speech:
*   **Wh- questions (What, Where, When):** Keep the question word, change the word order to Subject + Verb (no auxiliary 'do/does/did').
    *   Direct: "Where **do you live**?" he asked.
    *   Reported: He asked me where **I lived**.
*   **Yes/No questions:** Use "if" or "whether".
    *   Direct: "**Are you** happy?" she asked.
    *   Reported: She asked me **if I was** happy.

## 4. Link Words (The Connectors)

Connecting ideas logically.

*   **Cause (السبب):** Because, since, as, because of (+ noun), due to (+ noun).
*   **Result (النتيجة):** So, therefore, consequently, as a result, so...that, such...that.
*   **Concession/Contrast (التناقض):** Although, even though, though, despite (+ noun/v-ing), in spite of (+ noun/v-ing), however, but.
*   **Condition (الشرط):** If, provided that, as long as, unless (if not).
