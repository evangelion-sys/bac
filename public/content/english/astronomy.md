## 1. Key Vocabulary (The Cosmic Words)

To navigate the cosmos, you need the right terminology!

### The Celestial Bodies (الأجرام السماوية)
*   **The Universe / Cosmos (الكون):** All existing matter and space considered as a whole.
*   **Galaxy (مجرة):** A system of millions or billions of stars, together with gas and dust, held together by gravitational attraction. (Our galaxy is the Milky Way - درب التبانة).
*   **Solar System (النظام الشمسي):** The collection of eight planets and their moons in orbit round the sun, together with smaller bodies in the form of asteroids, meteoroids, and comets.
*   **Star (نجم):** A fixed luminous point in the night sky which is a large, remote incandescent body like the sun.
*   **Planet (كوكب):** A celestial body moving in an elliptical orbit round a star.
*   **Moon / Satellite (قمر / قمر صناعي):** A natural satellite of any planet.
*   **Asteroid (كويكب):** A small rocky body orbiting the sun.
*   **Comet (مذنب):** A celestial object consisting of a nucleus of ice and dust and, when near the sun, a ‘tail’ of gas and dust particles pointing away from the sun.
*   **Meteor / Shooting Star (نيزك / شهاب):** A small body of matter from outer space that enters the earth's atmosphere, becoming incandescent as a result of friction and appearing as a streak of light.

### Space Exploration (استكشاف الفضاء)
*   **Astronaut / Cosmonaut (رائد فضاء):** A person who is trained to travel in a spacecraft.
*   **Spacecraft / Spaceship (مركبة فضائية):** A vehicle used for travelling in space.
*   **Space Shuttle (مكوك فضائي):** A rocket-launched spacecraft, able to land like an unpowered aircraft, used to make repeated journeys between the earth and earth orbit.
*   **Telescope (تلسكوب):** An optical instrument designed to make distant objects appear nearer.
*   **Orbit (مدار):** The curved path of a celestial object or spacecraft round a star, planet, or moon.
*   **Gravity (الجاذبية):** The force that attracts a body towards the centre of the earth, or towards any other physical body having mass.

## 2. Essential Grammar (The Cosmic Spells)

These grammar structures are frequently tested in the "Language Outcomes" section.

### 1. Conditional Sentences (If clauses)
These express a condition and its result.

*   **Type 0 (General Truths / Scientific Facts):**
    *   *Rule:* If + **Present Simple**, **Present Simple**
    *   *Example:* If you **heat** ice, it **melts**. (Scientific fact).
*   **Type 1 (Possible Future Situations):**
    *   *Rule:* If + **Present Simple**, **will + stem** (Future)
    *   *Example:* If it **rains** tomorrow, I **will stay** home. (A real possibility).
*   **Type 2 (Unreal/Imaginary Present Situations):**
    *   *Rule:* If + **Past Simple**, **would + stem** (Conditional Present)
    *   *Example:* If I **were** an astronaut, I **would travel** to Mars. (I am not an astronaut now).
*   **Type 3 (Unreal Past Situations / Regrets):**
    *   *Rule:* If + **Past Perfect** (had + past participle), **would have + past participle** (Conditional Past)
    *   *Example:* If I **had studied** harder, I **would have passed** the exam. (I didn't study, so I didn't pass).

### 2. Expressing Certainty and Probability (Modals)
*   **Certainty (اليقين):** Must (must be true), Can't (cannot be true).
    *   *Example:* He has a telescope; he **must** be interested in astronomy.
*   **Probability/Possibility (الاحتمال):** May, Might, Could.
    *   *Example:* There **might** be life on other planets.

## ✨ The Active Recall Test

Let's test your cosmic knowledge, Serine! Try to answer before revealing the magic solution.

**Question 1 :** Complete the sentence with the correct verb tense:
*If scientists (to discover) water on Mars, they (to send) a manned mission.*
<details>
  <summary>Click for the Solution Magic ✨</summary>
  
  **Solution :**
  This is a Type 1 conditional (a possible future situation).
  If scientists **discover** (Present Simple) water on Mars, they **will send** (Future Simple) a manned mission.
</details>

**Question 2 :** What is the difference between an "Asteroid" and a "Comet"?
<details>
  <summary>Click for the Solution Magic ✨</summary>
  
  **Solution :**
  - **Asteroid:** A small rocky body orbiting the sun (mostly found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter).
  - **Comet:** A celestial object made of ice and dust that develops a glowing "tail" when it gets close to the sun.
</details>
